## iOS 14
* Home screen updates
  * App Library. Auto organizes apps at the end of your home screen pages
  * Hide app pages you don't need
  * Updated widgets. Multiple sizes. **Add to home screens**
  * Smart stack widget: Auto switches between relevant widget through the day
* Picture in picture for playing videos, regardless of app.
* Siri
  * No longer full screen, just overlays
  * Run dictation on device
  * **Translate app**. Offline, basically kills *iTranslate converse*
* Messages
  * Pinned conversations
  * Memoji updates, new options
  * Groups
    * Threaded replies
    * Mentions
    * Updated group header, custom icons
* Maps
  * Updated in more countries
  * Guides
  * Cycling directions, in specific cities (NY, LA, SF)
  * Electric vehicles: tracks car charge and factors in charging stations
  * Congestion centers and green zones
* Car key support in car play (only 2020 bmw so far)
  * Disable via iCloud
  * Share via messages, can set restricted driving profiles
* App Clips
  * Light and fast mini-apps, discovered from safari, messages, NFC tags, QR codes, Appclip codes
  * Less than 10MB, part of an app, built with xcode

## Apple Silicon
Dropping intel proccessors, moving to internal apple silicon.
A4 through A13 iphone chips focused on performance per watt.
Mac will do the same.

* "Universal 2" Single binary that will work on intel and Apple Silicon
* "Rosetta 2" Will drive intel apps on macs with apple silicon
  * Translates at install time
  * Support for JIT
  * Transparent to users
* New virtualization technologies?
* iPhone and iPad apps will run natively
* Developer quick start program, including hardware

Expect to ship by end of the year, transition to take about 2 years.
Expect to continue to ship updates for intel macs for "years to come".
Even still have new intel hardware updates still in the pipeline.
